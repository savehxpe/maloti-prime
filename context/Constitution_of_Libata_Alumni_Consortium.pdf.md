### CONSTITUTION OF LIBATA ALUMNI CONSORTIUM (An Initiative of Former Students of Masitise High School) 

1. Introduction 

The Libata Alumni Consortium is a structured and legally compliant initiative established by 

former students of Masitise High School. Recognizing the need to actively contribute to the 

advancement of our alma mater while simultaneously creating sustainable economic 

opportunities for ourselves, we have resolved to unite under a formal consortium model. 

This consortium serves a dual purpose: 

1. To generate lawful and sustainable income for its members, and 

2. To support Masitise High School in achieving academic excellence and 

infrastructure development. 

The consortium operates on principles of integrity, accountability, transparency, unity, and 

long-term sustainability. 

2. Vision 

To build a financially sustainable alumni-driven investment consortium that empowers its 

members economically while restoring and strengthening Masitise High School as a center of 

academic excellence and infrastructural development. 

3. Mission 

To mobilize financial and human resources among former students. 

To invest in profitable ventures that ensure long-term financial growth. 

To support Masitise High School through academic programs, infrastructure 

development, and resource mobilization. 

To create a structured, transparent, and professionally managed alumni investment 

platform.

4. Core Objectives 

The consortium aims to: 

1. Establish profitable investment projects. 

2. Acquire, develop, and manage income-generating properties. 

3. Support school infrastructure projects such as classrooms, laboratories, libraries, and 

sports facilities. 

4. Sponsor academic excellence initiatives including scholarships, awards, tutoring 

programs, and career guidance. 

5. Promote networking and professional collaboration among alumni. 

5. Fundraising and Revenue Generation Strategies 

To ensure diversified and sustainable income streams, the consortium will engage in the 

following activities: 

5.1 Annual Membership Contributions 

Each registered member shall contribute a minimum of R10,000.00 per financial year until 

the desired capital is reached. Members may contribute more, and higher contributions will 

proportionally increase their revenue-sharing ratios. 

5.2 Agricultural Ventures 

The consortium will engage in commercial agriculture, including: 

Crop production 

Livestock farming 

Poultry production 

Horticulture and greenhouse farming 

Agricultural products may be sold locally or regionally to generate steady income. 

5.3 Property Investment 

A significant portion of the pooled funds will be invested in: 

Development of new residential or commercial properties 

Purchase of existing income-generating properties 

Rental properties and long-term real estate investments

Concerts 

Alumni dinners and galas

6. Financial Year 

The consortium’s financial year shall commence on 1 April and conclude on 31 March of 

the following year. 

Annual financial statements will be prepared and presented at the Annual General Meeting 

(AGM). 

7. Membership Structure 

7.1 Eligibility 

Priority membership is reserved for former students of Masitise High School. 

Exceptions may be made for individuals who possess rare skills, high technical 

expertise, or exceptional talents that significantly benefit the consortium. 

7.2 Rights of Members 

Members shall: 

Participate in meetings. 

Vote on key decisions. 

Receive proportional revenue shares. 

Access consortium financial reports. 

7.3 Exit Policy 

Members who voluntarily exit the consortium: 

Shall not be refunded their contributions. 

Forfeit their financial rights within the consortium. 

In the event of death or serious disability: 

A legally appointed heir shall assume the membership position. 

The heir will inherit all associated rights and obligations.

8. Governance and Management 

The consortium will operate under a well-structured governance framework comprising: 

Chairperson 

Vice Chairperson 

Secretary 

Treasurer 

Investment and Projects Committee 

Disciplinary and Dispute Resolution Committee 

8.1 Meetings 

Quarterly Meetings: To review progress, investments, and operational matters. 

Annual General Meeting (AGM): To present audited financial reports, declare 

revenue distribution, and review strategic direction. 

8.2 Day-to-Day Management 

The executive committee will oversee: 

Operational decisions 

Financial management 

Project implementation 

Conflict resolution 

All decisions will be documented and subject to internal accountability standards. 

9. Revenue Distribution 

Revenue generated from investments shall: 

Be distributed according to each member’s contribution ratio. 

Be declared at the end or beginning of the financial year after financial reconciliation. 

Only be distributed once obligations, reserves, and reinvestment allocations have been 

accounted for. 

This proportional distribution model ensures fairness and prevents skewed allocation of 

resources.

10. Accountability and Transparency 

To maintain trust and credibility: 

Financial records shall be properly maintained. 

Independent audits may be conducted annually. 

All major financial decisions will require committee approval. 

Members shall have access to financial statements. 

11. Long-Term Sustainability Strategy 

The consortium will prioritize: 

Reinvestment of a portion of annual profits. 

Diversification of investments. 

Risk management strategies. 

Strategic partnerships. 

Professional financial advisory where necessary. 

12. Commitment to Masitise High School 

A defined percentage of annual profits or agreed project allocations will directly support: 

Academic performance programs 

Infrastructure rehabilitation and development 

Educational materials and equipment 

Scholarships for deserving students 

The ultimate goal is to restore and maintain Masitise High School as a leading institution of 

excellence. 

Conclusion 

The Libata Alumni Consortium represents a bold, structured, and forward-thinking initiative 

that merges alumni unity with economic empowerment and social responsibility. Through 

disciplined investment, transparent governance, and collective commitment, the consortium 

seeks to create sustainable wealth for its members while making a lasting and measurable 

impact on Masitise High School for generations to come.
