Summary 

Lesotho was the first African country to legalize cannabis cultivation for medical and 

scientific use. While the legal industry operates under government licensing, there is no 

official program for domestic medical cannabis access or for tourists. Entry with medical 

cannabis is not formally regulated, and travelers are advised to exercise extreme caution. 

Background 

Lesotho legalized the cultivation, processing, and export of medical cannabis in 2017. 

The licensing system is overseen by the Lesotho Ministry of Health, which authorizes 

producers and exporters, mostly for international pharmaceutical markets. 

Despite being a leader in cannabis exportation, there is no domestic patient access system 

or recognition of foreign medical cannabis prescriptions. 

The local use of cannabis for medical or recreational purposes remains criminalized, though 

laws are inconsistently enforced. 

Non-Medical Cannabis 

Consumption: Illegal, though culturally widespread in rural areas. Use is not formally 

tolerated. 

Possession: Prohibited without license. Small amounts can lead to fines or detention. 

Sale & Distribution: Only licensed producers may cultivate or export. Unlicensed sale is a 

criminal offense. 

Traveling to Lesotho with Medical Cannabis 

There is no formal process or authorization that allows travelers to legally bring 

cannabis-based medications into Lesotho.

Even though Lesotho supports medical cannabis production, possession by individuals, 

citizens or foreigners, is still considered illegal unless part of a licensed operation. 

Steps to Take Before Departure: 

1. Do not attempt to bring medical cannabis products into Lesotho, including oils, 

edibles, or dried flower. 

2. Speak with your doctor about non-cannabis alternatives for your condition during 

your stay. 

3. If your treatment is essential, consult the Lesotho embassy or consulate to confirm 

current laws or temporary accommodation (though unlikely). 

4. Carry a doctor’s letter and prescription copy for any non-cannabis medication you 

bring. 

5. If arriving from or transiting through countries with strict narcotics enforcement, 

ensure your luggage is compliant throughout your route. 

Practical Tips 

Do not rely on Lesotho’s export status as justification for bringing in cannabis, licensing is strictly industrial and does not apply to individuals. 

CBD products may also be restricted if they contain any THC, check ingredient labels carefully. 

There is no formal Schengen-style declaration or tolerance policy for imported cannabis medications. 

If you require cannabis-based treatment, consider delaying travel or coordinating remote care. 

The information provided reflects the most current data available; however, as cannabis laws are continually evolving, this content should not be interpreted as legal advice.
